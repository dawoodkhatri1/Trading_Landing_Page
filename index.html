History

1. Early Barter Systems: Began with direct exchanges of goods and services, limited by the need for mutual wants.
2. Emergence of Currency: Introduction of commodity money (e.g., shells, gold) around 3000 BCE facilitated trade.
3. Development of Markets: Ancient civilizations established markets and trade routes like the Silk Road.
4. Middle Ages: Rise of merchant classes and banking systems enhanced long-distance trade.
5. Age of Exploration: European colonial trade networks expanded global commerce.
6. Industrial Revolution: Innovations in transportation and the establishment of stock exchanges transformed trading.
7. 20th Century: Growth of globalization and electronic trading platforms.
